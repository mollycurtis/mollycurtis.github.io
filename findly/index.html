01 — Introduction ✦

Who is Findly?

/fīnd·lee/ — like "find" + "ly" · not "fin-dlee"

"Found, not bought."

Findly curates vintage home décor for people who believe a room should feel found, not bought. Share an inspiration board and a real person sources the pieces — handpicked, secondhand, and deliberately chosen.

Curated Vintage Mid-Century Intentional Warm
01

The Find Over the New

Mass-produced décor fills a room. Found décor furnishes a life. Findly exists in opposition to furniture that arrives flat-packed and forgotten in five years.

02

Personal, Not Generic

Every curation starts with the customer's inspo board. A real person reads it. A real person does the sourcing. No dropshipping, no catalogue, no shortcuts.

03

Mid-Century as a Lens

The aesthetic runs warmly through the 1940s–70s. Organic shapes, earthy tones, materials that improve with age. Not a period piece — a sensibility.

04

Sustainable by Default

Everything Findly sources already exists. No new manufacturing. No virgin materials. The most sustainable piece of furniture is one that already has a life behind it.

02 — How It Works

Simple. Personal. Surprising.

Choose your tier

Pick the curation size that fits your space and budget — from a small accent haul to a full room sourcing run. Or go monthly with The Find.

02

03

04

Your finds arrive

Wrapped in kraft paper, with piece notes on each item — origin, era, material, why it was chosen. Unbox it. Put it somewhere good.

04 — Colors ✦

A palette of warm rooms

Teak wood, burnt orange tile, aged ceramic, olive drapes. The palette of a well-loved mid-century living room — earthy, grounded, and deliberately warm. No cool tones. No grey.

05 — Typography

Slab, italic, clean.

Alfa Slab One for display presence. Cormorant Garamond for editorial soul. Josefin Sans for legibility and structure. Each has a lane — cross them intentionally or not at all.

06 — Imagery ✦

Warm light. Real texture.

Photography looks like it was shot on a Saturday morning in someone's actual living room. Amber light, linen surfaces, things slightly out of place. If it looks like a catalogue, it's wrong for Findly.

```
Afternoon amber light
Linen + ceramic close-up
Vintage teak detail
Open box, kraft paper
Layered textiles, floor
Object provenance tag
01

Amber & warm

Morning window light, tungsten lamps, golden late afternoon. Warm grading always. No studio lighting, no cool-toned overexposure.

02

Tactile & close

Shoot close enough to feel the grain of teak, the weave of a throw, the glaze of a ceramic. Texture is the product — show it.

03

Contextual, lived-in

No white studio floors. Objects in real rooms, on linen surfaces, with natural context. A room in use looks better than a room on display.

```
