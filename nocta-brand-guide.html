01 — Introduction ✦

Who is NOCTA?

NOCTA — short for nocturnal, built for after dark. A wood-fired pizza brand that only exists between 8PM and 4AM, for the city's creatives, night-shift workers, and anyone who believes the best decisions happen after dark. We don't do lunch. We don't do beige. We don't do sorry.

DarkUnapologetic NocturnalArtisanBold

Nightlife, Not Late-Night

NOCTA doesn't apologize for the hour. We are the hour. Our brand lives in low light, smoke, and the sound of a city that refuses to sleep.

Craft Without Rules

Every pie is made by hand. Every topping combination is deliberate chaos. The dough is disciplined. The toppings aren't.

Community of the After-Hours

Chefs, bartenders, musicians, designers. NOCTA belongs to the people who build the culture everyone else consumes in the daytime.

Tone of Voice
Confident & direct
"Eight toppings. Zero apologies. Open until 4."
No softening language. Short declarations. Periods, not ellipses.
"We think you might really enjoy our interesting range of unique pizza flavors!"
Darkly playful
"The fig and gorgonzola was a terrible idea. We're keeping it."
Humor with an edge. Never silly. Never corporate-casual.
"Fun for the whole family! Great for parties and events!"
Nocturnal & specific
"The wood burns hotter at 2AM. We don't know why. We don't ask questions."
Lean into the night. Be exact. Time, temperature, texture — name it.
"Fresh ingredients sourced with love for a great taste experience."
Never: cheerful, soft, corporate
"We're closed during daylight hours. Some of us burn."
No exclamation marks. No "amazing." No "we're passionate about."
"We're so excited to share our passion for pizza with your community!! 🍕🔥"
02 — Logo

The mark

NOCTA in Bebas Neue — all-caps, tight, unmistakable. No symbol, no icon, no tagline required. The name carries its own weight.

Primary — Wordmark
NOCTA Pizza After Dark

Uppercase, always. On Black, Cream, or Red, always. No exceptions, no variations. If it needs explaining, the context is wrong — not the logo.

Usage on backgrounds
NOCTA On Black — Primary
NOCTA On Cream — Print only
NOCTA On Red — Accents only
Clear Space & Minimum Size
1× cap height 1× cap height 1× 1× NOCTA
Min. digital

Wordmark at 72px width minimum on screens. Below this, letterform clarity suffers.

Min. print

Minimum 22mm print width. On packaging, never below 14mm in single color.

Clear space

Always 1× cap-height on all four sides, minimum. No adjacent text or elements.

✓ Do

Always uppercase. Use on Black, Cream, or Red only. Maintain clear space. Lock proportions. Let the name work on its own.

✗ Don't

Never skew, italicize, or outline the wordmark. Never place on purple, blue, or photo backgrounds. Never lowercase.

Flame is the signal

All CTAs, key highlights, box lid stripe. It's the fire in the oven. Use it where you need the eye to go — nowhere else.

Gold is the crust

Warm, earned, secondary. Use for prices, secondary labels. Never compete with Flame. One warm accent at a time.

Parchment is the paper

Menu backgrounds, receipt stock, flyers, print. Never a dominant digital color — NOCTA lives in the dark.

04 — Typography

Three voices, one vibe

Bebas Neue shouts. DM Serif whispers with a smirk. Barlow gets the work done. Each has its lane — cross them intentionally or not at all.

Display — Bebas Neue
THE PIE AFTER LAST CALL

All headlines, signage, packaging labels. Always uppercase. Tracking at 0.08–0.18em. Use for anything that needs to be read from across a dark room.

Accent — DM Serif Display
"wood-fired & unrepentant"

Pull quotes, subheadings, menu category labels. Always italic. Always in Flame or Parchment. The contrast to Bebas — emotion against structure.

Body — Barlow / Barlow Condensed
Barlow 400 — regular copy
Condensed 600 — labels

All body text, menu descriptions, app UI, captions. Barlow for prose, Barlow Condensed for labels, tags, navigation.

05 — Imagery ✦

Dark, textured, real

Photography looks like it was shot at 1AM with available light. Warm, imperfect, honest. If it looks too polished, it's wrong for NOCTA.

Moodboard Direction

01

Low & warm light

Tungsten, fire, neon. Shadows are welcome. High contrast is on brand. Never use studio-bright overhead lighting.

02

Dark, warm palette

Browns, reds, near-blacks. The occasional gold from cheese or crust. No cool tones. No food-magazine blue-whites.

03

Imperfect & human

Action shots over still life. Flour on hands. A slice mid-pull. The oven door open. Imperfection is authenticity for NOCTA.
